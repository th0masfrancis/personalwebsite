Amazon’s Australian Problem

Words like community engagement and local integration rarely find top priority in marketing playbooks. For Amazon, the American online retail juggernaut this ‘local’ integration may be the missing piece in the puzzle to dominate Australia. In this country, which is a proudly immigrant nation, cultural integration is one of the key factors for success, be it a newly arrived immigrant or a corporation which is trying to gain market share.

Australian consumers are famous for preferring ‘local’ corporations over ‘foreign’ companies. Performance of Starbucks in this coffee drinking country is a clear evidence. Amazon’s lackluster performance in Australia should be analyzed under this light.

Key factors which are slowing down Amazon’s growth in the continent

1. Lack of Word of Mouth Uptake – Close-knit communities and regular social gatherings among the middle-aged population (Australian median age 37) means that word of mouth carries a lot of weight in an Australian consumer’s decision on where to make retail purchases from. Amazon is absent from these barbeque side conversations.

2. Incumbent Network Effects – E-bay is the most popular online marketplace and has the largest number of customers and sellers. Driving the current buyers and sellers away from this platform and into Amazon’s fold will be an uphill task.

3. Insufficient Localization of Content – Even though English speaking, Australian’s are culturally distinct from the rest of the world. The prime programming (movies and music) should reflect this distinctness and feature more local content. Featuring INXS on Prime homepage is a step in the right direction.

4. Lack of Community Engagement – A more offline approach, by sponsoring local events like NFL and engaging with local communities will help in integration of Amazon into local communities.
